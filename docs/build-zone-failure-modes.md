gen-p5-t2-rot_180-s1, gen-p5-t2-rot_180-s7, and gen-p5-t2-rot_180-s13 share the same issues. the spawn piece looks visually too small, a 1x2 piece. on all three seeds there is a large bridge region ahead of the spawn. for these small maps it does not make much sense to have these, it slows spawning players too much. the wool in all three examples is at the same place, the lane is completely parallel to the connecting piece. here an offset AWAY from mid would be better. 

the next issue in the three seeds (which continues to all other seeds really) is that the build region in the middle cover way too much and there are multiple overlapping regions. the mid build zone should never interface with the wool piece, especially not literally include the wool spawn position. otherwise players can just bridge through the mid freely into the point and there is no gameplay direction whatsoever. map designers often intentionally extrude a part of a lane to form an overlap or L or T-section (exactly what would happen with moving the wool lane back) where a build region can visually dock against. this also makes layouts very readable. having the complete mid covered in one big build zone that spans the whole width of the map AND more is not the way. all other seed families shares the same issue with oversized mid build regions and overlap with the frontline regions when it should dock / snap against them. gen-p12-t2-rot_180-s7 also has the build region overlap with the pieces while it should just sit between them. it is also a great example where the build region is the same width as the wool lane and is at least the correct with. BUT the interface / overlap with the main land is a bad smell not just because of the overlap. the interface is 2 cells wide but runs along a straight edge that is at least 8 cells WITH NO small 2 wide "connector piece" that indicates the "offspring" from the large island. the connection point into large lands requires this. basically a bridge region requires readability. i have created a new sketch highlighting valid build interfaces and their invalid counterparts.

build-interface-dos-and-donts.

piece <-> zone <-> piece-2: zone is a build zone that connects piece and piece-2 by running parallel against their ends. in this specific case it is the mid build zone that connects the two teams. it also creates a rotation hole with piece, piece-2 and piece-3. a negative example of this pattern is visualized in zone-5, piece-4, piece-5, and piece-10. here zone-5 overlaps with piece-4 and piece-5 creating a small build region that can not really be explained and does not make sense. 

piece-10 <-> zone-5: the interface between piece-10 and zone-5 spans the full width of piece-10. a good example and rule. 

piece-6 <-> zone-2 <-> piece-3: piece-3 creates a small extrusion from the lane it offsprings from which is made from piece and piece-4. it also has the perfect width to bridge the gap and no overlap with other pieces. the negative example is piece-3 <-> piece-7 <-> wool-2 where zone-3 borders the 8-long-lane formed by piece-2, piece-3, and piece-5. this makes it hard to read. i did also intentionally make zone-3 larger than the actual required 2x2 piece to show bad piece and void overlap which is a common theme inside the generated corpus.
